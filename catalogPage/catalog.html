Geospatial Computing

More than just GIS

In my opinion, the Geospatial Computing degree gives you all of the benefits of an Interdisciplinary degree (studying topics from a broad, but connected, range of fields, lots of options as a graduate entering the workforce, etc) but without any of the less desirable consequences (a degree that may seem unfocused).

As a student, I got to learn geology, GIS, remote sensing, chemistry, statistics, calculus, and several programming languages. You also get to earn a supplemental certificate in a focus area of your choice, such as system administration, Computer-aided drafting, data science, or networking. This degree has many options available so you can customize it to your own personal taste and career goals.
